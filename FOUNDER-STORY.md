# Why I Built DeckShark

I've been playing Commander for 8 years. I have 14 decks. I actively play maybe 10 of them. The rest are sleeved, boxed, and sitting on a shelf.

Last year I tried selling one on Facebook Marketplace. It sat there for two months. The guy who finally showed up got the deck, the sleeves, and the deckbox for 70% of what the cards alone were worth. That's the best-case scenario right now.

The alternatives aren't better. Your LGS will give you store credit at a loss, or cash at an even bigger loss. You can try to part it out, but nobody's meeting up to buy your Sol Ring. You end up selling the five chase cards and sitting on the rest. And if you're lucky, someone in your pod might want to swap, but that almost never lines up.

There's just no good way to trade a full deck right now.

That's why I built DeckShark. You paste your decklist, every card gets priced automatically, and your deck shows up for local players to browse. Someone nearby wants it? They propose a trade, you meet at your LGS or a coffee shop, and you both walk away with something new. A deck you can shuffle up and play that same night. No parting out, no shipping singles, no waiting.

I work in cybersecurity by day and build DeckShark at night. It's just me, and it's free.

If you're one of the first 500 people to sign up, you get a permanent "Founding Member" badge on your profile. That's not just a label. You're literally helping build this thing from the ground up, and I want everyone to know you were here first. Seriously, thank you for taking a chance on something new.

The dream? I'm sitting down at a new pod, nobody knows I built DeckShark, and someone across the table says "have you checked DeckShark for that?" That's the moment I'm building toward. I genuinely believe we can get there, and every person who signs up and lists a deck is helping make it real.
